GulabJamun

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour &1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3-4 tbsp milk &oil or ghee for frying
  • 1.5 cups sugar &2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp rose water

Recipe:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Mix well.
  2. Add ghee and mix until the mixture resembles bread crumbs.
  3. Add milk little by little and knead the mixture into a soft dough.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  5. In a wide pot, add sugar and water. Heat on a medium flame until the sugar dissolves. Then, let the syrup boil until it reaches a sticky consistency. Add cardamom powder and rose water. Mix well and turn off the heat.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into smooth balls
  7. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Once the oil is hot, reduce the flame to low and add the balls. Fry them on low flame until they turn golden brown.
  8. Remove the fried balls and add them to the sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Barfi

Ingredients:

  • Milk powder
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom

Recipe:

  1. Heat ghee in a nonstick pan. Add whole milk and bring this to a boil on low flame.
  2. Keep stirring it to prevent it from burning at the bottom.
  3. Once the milk starts bubbling, add milk powder in increments and keep whisking it so there are no lumps.
  4. Continue stirring until the mixture thickens or forms like mass, and ghee starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  5. Stop cooking once the mixture thickens or forms like mass, and ghee starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  6. Pour the mixture into a greased tray and let it cool down.
  7. Cut the barfi into desired shapes and sizes.Garnish with nuts or edible silver

Besan Ladoo

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup of ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 2/3 teaspoon of green cardamom powder
  • 4 tablespoons of chopped cashews (optional)

Recipe:

  1. Heat a pan on low flame and add the besan. Roast the besan until it turns aromatic and golden in color.
  2. Add the ghee and mix well. Continue to roast the mixture on low flame until the ghee starts to separate from the mixture.
  3. Turn off the flame and let the mixture cool down for a few minutes.
  4. Add the sugar and cardamom powder to the mixture and mix well.
  5. Add the chopped cashews and mix well.
  6. Take a small portion of the mixture and shape it into a ball. Repeat this process until all the mixture is used up.
  7. Store the besan ladoos in an airtight container.

Jalebi

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour and 1/2 cup corn flour
  • 2 cups hung curd
  • 1 1/2 pinch baking soda
  • 1/2 cup ghee and 2 cups sunflower oil
  • 3 cups sugar and 3 cups water
  • 5 strands saffron
  • 4 drops rose essence
  • 1/2 teaspoon green cardamom powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon edible food color

Recipe:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, corn flour, hung curd, and baking soda. Mix well.
  2. Add water to the mixture and whisk until smooth.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the mixture ferment for 10-12 hours.
  4. In a separate pan, heat the ghee and oil together.
  5. Pour the batter into a piping bag and pipe the batter into the hot oil in a spiral shape.
  6. Fry the jalebis until they turn golden brown.
  7. In a separate pan, combine the sugar, water, saffron, rose essence, and green cardamom powder. Cook until the sugar dissolves and the syrup thickens.
  8. Dip the fried jalebis in the sugar syrup for 2-3 minutes.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
